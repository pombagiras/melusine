Universal Terminology

Sacred Concepts

Axé

The sacred vital force and spiritual power that permeates everything, maintaining the balance of the universe.

Orixá

Nature deities representing natural forces and human aspects, such as Ogum (war), Iemanjá (seas), and Oxalá (creation).

Egregore

A collective energetic field formed by the thoughts, feelings, and beliefs of a specific group.

Ponto Riscado

A sacred drawing made with chalk (pemba) to invoke, identify, or anchor spiritual entities.

Curimba

The musical band of the temple responsible for the vibrations that sustain the spiritual ceremony.

Tronqueira

The energetic threshold where Exus and Pombagiras operate, usually represented by crossroads or gates.

Atabaque

Sacred drum of the curimba, responsible for marking the rhythm of the sung points and calling the entities. Made of wood and animal hide, its sound is considered a sonic offering that opens spiritual portals.

Médium

An individual who serves as a bridge between the physical and spiritual planes. The channel through which entities like Pombagiras, Exus, Caboclos, and Pretos-Velhos can transit and act in aid of humanity.

Truths

Is Pombagira a demon?
No. In Umbanda and Quimbanda, Pombagira is an evolved spiritual entity that works for good and karmic balance, acting in matters of justice and love.
Can I light a candle on my own?
It is not recommended without prior knowledge. It involves vibrational specifics that require a priest's guidance for your spiritual safety.
Why roses, champagne, and mirrors?
They symbolize love and beauty (Roses), the effervescence of life (Champagne), and truth/self-knowledge (Mirrors).
Do they punish people?
They do not act on a whim. They work with the law of return and spiritual justice. "Punishment" is merely the natural rebalancing against those who practiced injustice.
Relationship with the LGBTQIA+ community?
Historically, Afro-Brazilian religions were safe havens. By breaking gender and sexuality taboos, Pombagiras have become symbols of resistance and spiritual validation.
