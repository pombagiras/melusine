What Are the Signs of a Pombagira?

A Complete Guide for Those Seeking to Understand This Spiritual Connection by Alexia Melusine.

Few figures in Afro-Brazilian spirituality arouse as much curiosity as the Pombagiras.

Just look at the searches done daily on the internet. Thousands of people are looking for answers to questions like:

"Do I have a Pombagira?"
“Why do I feel so connected to them?”
"Can a Pombagira appear in dreams?"
"Can men also have Pombagira?"
"Can trans and transvestite people work with Pombagiras?"

The interesting thing is that these questions are rarely just about spirituality. Most of the time, they reflect something deeper. These are questions about identity, belonging and self-knowledge, translating the attempt to understand experiences that seem difficult to explain.

But after all, what does it really mean to feel a connection with a Pombagira?

The First Signs of the Presence of a Pombagira

One of the greatest expectations of those who begin this search is to find a definitive sign. However, according to numerous reports within Afro-Brazilian traditions, the approach of a Pombagira usually happens gradually. There is not always an extraordinary experience.

Often the process begins with unexpected curiosity. People start researching Pombagiras frequently. Feels interested in stories, sung points, images or symbols that previously went unnoticed. Some people report recurring dreams, while others notice changes in their intuition or a growing sense of emotional empowerment, self-esteem and independence.

This does not automatically mean the presence of a specific entity, but it may represent the beginning of a journey of spiritual investigation.

Does Every Woman Have a Pombagira?

This is one of the most widespread ideas and also one of the most misunderstood. There is no rule that states that every woman must have a Pombagira. Within Afro-Brazilian traditions, each spiritual trajectory is unique.

Some women develop mediumship linked to the Pombagiras; others work with different spiritual lines; and others simply admire these entities without any mediumistic relationship.

The association between women and Pombagiras happens because many of these entities symbolize aspects linked to feminine strength, autonomy, magnetism and resistance. But admiration is not the same thing as spiritual bonding.

Can a Man Have Pombagira?

Yes. And this is perhaps one of the answers that most surprises those who are beginning to study the subject. Pombagiras do not belong exclusively to the feminine universe.

Men can incorporate, develop and work spiritually with Pombagiras, in the same way that women can work with Exus. Spirituality is not limited by the gender categories used by society. The mediumistic relationship occurs due to spiritual affinity, religious development and individual trajectory, and not due to the person's gender identity.

What Does a Pombagira Do in Someone’s Life?

There is a popular misconception that reduces Pombagiras exclusively to love affairs. In reality, the activities attributed to these entities tend to be much broader.

Several practitioners report transformations related to:

  • Autoestima e coragem;
  • Independência emocional e autoconhecimento;
  • Proteção espiritual;
  • Libertação de relacionamentos destrutivos;
  • Reconstrução da confiança pessoal.

Therefore, many people describe the presence of Pombagiras as an invitation to transformation — not necessarily a comfortable transformation, but often a profound one.

Why Do Some People Feel Such a Strong Connection to Pombagiras?

There are several possibilities. In some cases, this connection may be related to mediumship. In others, it may arise from identification with what the Pombagiras represent: freedom, authenticity, courage, resistance and personal power.

Many people recognize in these figures aspects that they want to develop within themselves. Therefore, feeling strong admiration for a Pombagira does not automatically mean having a mediumistic connection. Sometimes the connection is spiritual, sometimes symbolic, sometimes emotional — and often it's a mixture of all of these.

Can Pombagira Appear in Dreams?

Dreams are among the most frequent reports when it comes to spirituality. Many people describe experiences involving red roses, unknown women, elegant dresses, mirrors, crossroads, dancing or nightlife.

However, an isolated dream can hardly be interpreted as confirmation of any spiritual link. The most important thing is to observe recurring patterns and understand the general context of your own experience. Dreams can have spiritual meanings, but they can also reflect psychological, emotional and symbolic processes.

How did Pombagira manifest itself before incorporation?

When there is incorporation mediumship, the process is normally gradual. Common reports include intense energetic sensations, emotional changes during spiritual work, awareness of presence, goosebumps, feelings of heat, stronger intuitions, or the spontaneous desire to move the body or dance.

But it is important to highlight something fundamental: none of these signs are exclusive to Pombagiras. Therefore, serious traditions emphasize the importance of mediumistic development accompanied by experienced leaders. Precipitation often generates more confusion than clarification.

Is admiring a Pombagira the same thing as working with her?

No. And understanding this difference avoids many mistakes. A person can deeply admire figures like Maria Padilha without having any mediumistic relationship with them.

Admiration arises from respect, identification or cultural and spiritual interest. Spiritual work involves commitment, study, development and responsibility within a religious tradition. Every person who works with a Pombagira probably admires them, but not every person who admires a Pombagira works with them.

Can Trans People, Transvestites, Non-Binary People, Crossdressers and Drag Queens Have Pombagiras?

Yes. This is one of the most important questions today. Afro-Brazilian spirituality has a long history of coexistence with human diversity.

Trans, transvestites and non-binary people can work with Pombagiras. Drag queens and crossdressers can also have a spiritual affinity with this line or develop mediumship with them. The presence of a Pombagira does not depend on the person's gender identity, in the same way that cisgender men can incorporate Pombagiras and women can work with Exus. Spirituality does not operate within the same boundaries established by society.

Why Do Many LGBT+ People Identify With Pombagiras?

Perhaps because the Pombagiras represent something that goes far beyond religion. They often symbolize survival in the face of prejudice, the courage to exist, the refusal to accept impositions and the freedom to build one's own identity.

Throughout history, countless marginalized people have found in these entities an image of strength, dignity and resistance. Therefore, it is not uncommon for LGBT+ people to feel a deep identification with the universe of Pombagiras. This identification can be spiritual, but it can also be symbolic, cultural and emotional.

The Most Important Question

After all these reflections, perhaps there is an even more relevant question. It's not: "Do I have a Pombagira?" nor "What's my Pombagira?"

Perhaps the deeper question is: "What is this search trying to show me about myself?"

Because many times the spiritual journey begins exactly like this. Not with an answer, but with a question that insists on remaining. And, in some cases, it is precisely in this search that the most important transformations occur.
